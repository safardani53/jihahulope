Nursing home hounded dementia patient's family over 15,000 fees for care she never received

Publish date: 2024-09-15

A NURSING home charged a dementia patient an extra £700 a week for care she never received, reveals a damning watchdog report.

Managers increased the vulnerable widow's fees by 140 per cent then threatened her son with legal action unless he coughed up almost £15,000.

Haresbrook Park home in Worcestershire hiked the fees from £500 a week to £1,200 two months after the council said it would no longer pay the bill.

The home claimed the 72-year-old woman - named as Mrs C - needed special one-to-one care.

Bosses also back-dated the bill with the higher charges.

But she did not need the extra care and managers produced little evidence she ever received the service, said the Local Government and Social Care Ombudsman.

The watchdog's report said: "It (the care home) back-dated the increase, something the ombudsman was particularly critical of as there was no evidence the care home provided any increased care for the back-dated period.

"It therefore charged a vulnerable woman several thousands of pounds for a service it never delivered.

"The home claimed the increase was due to the woman's care needs, but when challenged could provide the ombudsman with little evidence the extra care was either needed or delivered."

The ombudsman also found a string of failings by Worcestershire County Council, which illegally stopped funding the woman's care in February 2015.

Social workers ruled she was able to pay for her own care.

But she had no capacity to take decisions for herself and her son did not have legal authority to manage her finances. She died in August that year.

The ombudsman said social workers made no proper assessment of the care she needed and the council broke the law when it said she should pay for it herself.

The report said: "Had social workers assessed her needs properly from the outset, much of the distress caused to the family could have been avoided.

"Instead the council left the woman and her family to deal with the care home alone."

The local authority has since apologised and paid £1,000 compensation.

The ombudsman also ordered care home operator Capital Care Group to repay the £15,000 it wrongly charged.

Owner Manjeet Rai apologised to Mrs C's family.

She told the Daily Mail that Haresbrook Park now "clearly documents one to one care to provide evidence to the funder that this is still required and is being delivered as described".
