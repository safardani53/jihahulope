BBC Weather glitch sees area of Kent deposited in middle of the Channel - and Twitter explodes

Publish date: 2024-09-06

A BBC weather map that placed an area of Kent in the middle of the English Channel was mocked on Twitter today.

The glitch during the BBC South East forecast at 6.30am this morning relocated Romney Marsh from the coast to some 20 miles out at sea.

A further blunder saw the town of Hythe find itself underwater.

The gaffes were quickly seized upon by social media users.

Simon Witherington said on Twitter: "What's happening with your map? Hythe and Romney Marsh are in the English Channel!"

Jan Weeks wrote: "What happened to Romney Marsh? @Kate_Kinsella 6.30am this morning floating in the middle of the channel! @BBCBreakfast #UDI"

Kate Kinsella, who presents the weather, made a joke about the unfortunate mishap after fixing the faulty map.

She tweeted: "Phew. I rescued Romney Marsh from the Channel @fantanjan, also Hythe @wnomis . They were making a break for France!"

Locals have seen the funny side and have been cracking jokes all morning.

One comment on Facebook said: "It's a service stop for Boris's bridge across the channel."

Another user said: "Do I need my passport to get back?"

And residents of nearby New Romney found their relocation amusing.

Independent councillor for New Romney Susie Govett said the town had been renamed "Le Marsh du Romney" by one good-humoured local, adding: "This is our break for freedom, we are going for independence!"

Others social media users got in on the act with John Adey posting on Facebook: "Romney Marsh is to separate from the UK and remain in the EU."

Karl Butler said: "That's global warming for you."

It comes after BBC Breakfast had viewers in hysterics yesterday when their floor manager was forced to hide behind a sofa and follows former BBC weatherman Bill Giles blasting the Corporation's new-look weather graphics.
