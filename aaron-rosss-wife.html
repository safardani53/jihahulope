Aaron Ross Wife | Early Life, Family & Education

Sanya Richards-Ross was born Sanya Richards on February 26, 1985, in Kingston, Jamaica.

She is the daughter of father Archie Richards and mother, Sharon Richards.

Sanya also has a sister Shari Richards.

She represented her school Vaz Prep in the annual youth championships at the age of seven.

Her family moved to Fort Lauderdale, Florida, the United States when Sanya was twelve years old.

The reason for their move to the States was so that Sanya could attend American high school, increasing her odds of obtaining an athletic scholarship.

Sanya was an excellent student excelling both in her studies and sports. She graduated from St. Thomas Aquinas High School in 2002 with a cumulative 4.0 GPA.

She then attended the University of Texas, Austin, and graduated in 2005 with a degree in business.

Aaron Ross Wife | Career

Sanya Richards-Ross started running at the age of seven.

At St. Thomas Aquinas High School, she was pegged the USA Track and Field Youth Athlete of the Year and also the 2002 Gatorade National High School Girls Track and Field Athlete of the Year.

Similarly, she was a nine-time individual state champion at Aquinas with four 100-meter titles, one 400-meter title, and three 200-meter titles.

In 2003, Sanya represented the University of Texas and won the NCAA national championship in the 400 meters and 4 X 400 meters relay.

She qualified for her first Olympic team by running 400 meters in 49.89 seconds at the 2004 U.S Olympic trials. She placed second in the trial.

Likewise, some of her other notable accolades include being the 2005 world silver medalist, 2008 Olympic bronze medalist, 2009 world champion, and 2012 Olympic champion.

Her 2012 victory made her the second American woman to win the 400 meters at the Olympics Games.

Moreover, she is also a six-time U.S national champion in 400 meters.

Her thriving career ended in 2016 when she hung her boots. The same year Sanya was inducted into the Texas Track and Field Coaches Hall of Fame.

After her retirement, she dabbled her feet in a bunch of other stuff, including writing and acting.

About Aaron Ross

Aaron Ross is a former American football cornerback drafted by the New York Jets 20th overall in the first round of the 2007 NFL Draft.

Ross was one of seven Longhorns drafted by professional football teams in that draft.

He played colligate football at the University of Texas, where he won the Jim Thorpe Award.

He has two Super Bowl rings, Super Bowl XLII and Super Bowl XLVI, both with the Giants.

Aside from the New York Giants, Ross also played for the Baltimore Ravens and Jacksonville Jaguars.

Ross was released by Jaguars on March 8, 2013, and shortly after that signed a deal with the New York Giants on March 12, 2013.

However, with a season-ending back injury, he was placed on injured reserve after week 4 of the 2013 NFL season.

The following season, he signed a contract with the Baltimore Ravens.

Nevertheless, Ross’s that season ended when he tore his Achilles tendon on July 24, 2014.

For the following season, Ross signed with the Cleveland Brown on August 16, 2015, but the team released him just 15 days later, on August 31, 2015.
