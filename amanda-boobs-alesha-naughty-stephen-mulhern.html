Amanda Holden flashed her boobs at me and Alesha Dixon is 'very naughty', reveals Stephen Mulhern

Publish date: 2024-07-02

HE has worked alongside Holly Willoughby, Alesha Dixon and a host of other glamorous female telly stars in the business.

But former Britain’s Got More Talent presenter Stephen Mulhern says Amanda Holden is by far the naughtiest.

Talking exclusively to The Sun, Stephen, 43, says: “The thing with Amanda is, what you see is what you get.

“But it’s the things you don’t see — the stuff we have to take out of BGT — that will shock you.

“I’ve had a flash of her boobs and I ask myself the same question, ‘How did that come about?’

“But it was all legal. It was on telly and we had to cut it out.”

Stephen presented the ITV2 BGT spin-off series for 13 years until it was axed last year.

In that time he grew close to hosts Ant and Dec and all the judges.

He says: “Alesha appears slightly perfect and is very cool about what she does, but she is naughty — a lot naughtier than she makes out on that panel.”

After hosting CITV’s Finger Tips with Fearne Cotton in 2001, Stephen’s big breakthrough was on Saturday morning kids’ show Ministry Of Mayhem, alongside Michael Underwood — who has recently changed profession to become a teacher — as well as a young Holly Willoughby, in 2004.

The series, which was renamed Holly & Stephen’s Saturday Show- down, ran for two years until 2006.

Stephen says: “I speak to Holly a lot. We haven’t presented together for a while but we’re good friends and we make a good match.

“I may need to steal her from Phillip Schofield.”

Fans were shocked last October when it was revealed that Britain’s Got More Talent would not return for the 2020 series.

Stephen was just as upset.

He says: “The reality is it was 13 years of my life. It was an incredible experience. I loved it.

“Was I gutted when it went? Of course. I don’t believe any presenter wouldn’t be.

“The only bonus that I take from it is that I am pleased they didn’t get another presenter, as that would have been heartbreaking.

“And if they did, I would have loved to know who they would have got.”

Stephen, who has previously said Simon Cowell, David Walliams, Ant and Dec all disagreed with the decision, adds: “When BGMT did go, Amanda was one of the first to send me a text saying, ‘It’s just nuts and we are going to miss you lots’.

“I will remember that for a very long time.”

Stephen returns to screens this week with game show Rolling In It.

It is an entertainment format he knows well, having also presented Catchphrase, In For A Penny and Big Star’s Little Star over his 20 years in the business.

But he says his latest series is his favourite so far.

A game of chance, Rolling In It sees three contestants play alongside some of their favourite celebrities and rolling a coin down a moving conveyor belt, with big cash prizes up for grabs.

It will feature appearances from big names including comedian Jimmy Carr, Towie star turned presenter Mark Wright and reality TV favourite Gemma Collins.

‘I HAD GOOSEBUMPS'

Stephen, who cut his showbiz teeth as a Butlins Red Coat, says: “This is my favourite show without question, without a doubt.

“When it came to actually filming the show, I’ve seen every game show you can name.

“I walked off after the first episode and had goosebumps. I genuinely got emotional. I feel like this is a really special show. Especially at the time where we are all in now, no matter what job you do, you have been affected in some way.

“Whether it’s your personal or work life, it gives you time to reflect on stuff.”

With his 13 years as part of the BGT family, Stephen has seen the lots of the UK’s favourite presenting duo of Ant and Dec up close — and he says he would be eager to form his own double act.

And his top choices would be either Holly, 39, again or Emma Willis, 44.

He says: “I’ve been lucky enough to present with Emma. I’m trying to think if any co-presenter has been a nightmare. I’ve really lucked out there.

“Sometimes it’s nice to do it with someone else and other times it’s nice to do it on your own.”

But for his favourite career moment he chooses his Royal Variety Performance in 2014 in front of the Queen at the Victoria Palace Theatre in the West End.

He had previously performed there aged 20 in 1997 after winning The Big Big Talent Show, hosted by Jonathan Ross, with his magic act.

Bringing out his magic tricks again in 2014, Stephen shared a dressing room with Simon Cowell — and he reveals it was not all it was cracked up to be.

He jokes: “I was there on my lonesome and he has an entourage that is bigger than my family.”

‘I WOULD BEAT ANT AND DEC ON CATCHPHRASE’

But he would be happy to share a room with Ant and Dec.

As Catchphrase returns for another series, Stephen says he is eager to get the boys on for a charity special.

And he would be happy to take them on himself.

When asked if he would ever consider being a contestant on the show, he says: “The answer is yes.
