Hair salon stands out with rainbow-mermaid styles...so would YOU try any of these?

Publish date: 2024-07-26

The mermaid trend was predicted to be left behind in 2017, but it looks like it's here to stay after a salon in Australia proved that the fantasy look is still all the rage.

Mermaid Salon in Brisbane went viral in May last year when they released their Chubby Mermaid make-up brush, which sent beauty bloggers and enthusiasts into meltdown.

Salon employee Kendal told the Daily Mail: "We love helping people achieve their dream hair goals. Our salon has been running for two years so we're still a baby.

"But we've recently moved to a bigger space so we can fit more clients in."

The colourful creations have earned the brand more than 20,000 followers on their Instagram page.

Kendal said: "We believe in helping people be their most authentic self, whether it's long rainbow locks or a complete shaved head.

"We also believe having a safe space, accepting of everyone and we only use vegan and cruelty-free products."

Alongside hairdressing and styling, they also sell make-up products online and in store, where makeup artists help complete the mythical looks.

Kendal explained the tools used to dye their clients' hair: "As far as the actual mermaid hair we do, we use Manic Panic dyes and often mix different colours to get the perfect shade."

The salon owners hope to challenge beauty standards and personal identity with their expressive designs and styles.

After their Chubby Mermaid Crave brush became an internet sensation, Fanny Barlow, the woman behind the brushes, had to work day and night to keep up with their popularity.

She previously told Vogue:  "I've actually paid extra to have them rush produced because I only had a couple of hundred at first and they sold out in six minutes and I just freaked out and texted my manufacturer straight away.

"I had another batch quickly made and I probably sold an extra two or three thousand. I can't believe it."

The success of the salon and its products proves that the mermaid obsession isn't about to go away any time soon.
