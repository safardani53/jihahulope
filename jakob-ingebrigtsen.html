Early Life And Family

Jakob Ingebrigtsen was born in Sandnes, Norway, on September 19, 2000. He grew up in a big family with six siblings; Henrik, Filip, Kristoffer, Martin, Ingrid, and William.

His father, Gjert Ingebrigtsen, is his coach and has won awards for his coaching. Their mother, Tone, works as a project manager at a hospital.

Despite their busy athletic careers, the Ingebrigtsen family has remained close-knit, with a deep sense of mutual support and encouragement.

This is evident in their TV show, Team Ingebrigtsen, which provides a glimpse into their daily lives and their challenges on and off the track.

Details On His Brothers, Henrik, and Filip

Jakob Ingebrigtsen comes from a family of runners, with his two older brothers, Henrik and Filip, also being accomplished middle-distance runners.

Henrik is the oldest brother and competes mainly in the 1500 meters. He has represented Norway at the Olympics and has won medals at the European Championships multiple times.

His personal best for the 1500m is 3:31.46, which is also a former Norwegian record.

Filip, the second oldest brother, competes mainly in the 1500 meters. He has represented Norway at the Olympics and won medals at the European and World Championships. His best for the 1500m is 3:30.01, a current Norwegian record.

All three brothers train together with their father Gjert.

Jakob Ingebrigtsen: Into The Tracks

Jakob Ingebrigtsen is a Norwegian middle-distance and long-distance runner who has achieved remarkable success in his career.

He began running at a young age and created a name for himself right away. At the age of 16, he was the youngest man to run a mile in under four minutes.

He has won numerous European championships and six European Cross Country titles. In addition, he has achieved success on the global stage, winning a gold medal in the 1500 meters at the 2020 Tokyo Olympics.

Further, he also won a gold medal in the 5000 meters and a silver medal in the 1500 meters at the 2022 World Athletics Championships.

He also holds the world indoor record in the 1500 meters and Norwegian records in several events.

Relationship, Wife, and Kids

Jakob Ingebrigtsen is not yet married but is engaged to Elisabeth Asserson, a runner. The couple announced their engagement in April 2021 after dating for five years.

They have not yet started a family but are parents to two dogs, Maximus and Jupiter. The couple often shares photos of themselves and their furry friend on social media.

Elisabeth Asserson is a middle and long-distance runner with impressive personal bests of 4:19.86 for the 1500 meters and 9:32.65 for the 3000 meters.

In addition to her running career, she is a popular public figure on Instagram, sharing glimpses of her life, running, and fashion with her followers.

She has also worked as a model, collaborating with brands and magazines.

Interesting Facts

  • Jakob is the second youngest-ever individual senior European track and field champion, winning the 1500 meters in 2018 at 17 years and 324 days. The first youngest-ever individual senior European track and field champion was Mari Cruz Diaz of Spain, who won the 10km race walk in 1986 at 16 years and 306 days. 
  • He is the first man to win the 1500 meters and 5000 meters double at the European Championships, which he did in 2018 and 2022.
  • He is the first male athlete to win the 1500 meters and 3000 meters double at the European Indoor Championships, which he did in 2021 and 2023.

Charity

Ingebrigtsen is involved in helping others through charitable activities. He supports Right To Play, which is an organization that helps children in need by using sports and games to empower them.

Likewise, Jakob is also an ambassador for a foundation called Aktiv mot Kreft, which encourages physical activity for people affected by cancer.

Further, he has participated in fundraising events for different causes, including mental health, climate change, and coronavirus relief.

Earnings, Endorsements, & Net Worth

Jakob Ingebrigtsen’s net worth is estimated to be between $4 million and $6 million, which he has earned mainly from his professional running career.
