Inside the biggest soft play mums are going wild for with laser tag, zip lines AND the UKs talles

Publish date: 2024-05-10

DID you know there is a colossal indoor soft play centre with zip lines, laser tag AND the UK’s tallest indoor slide not far from Manchester?

Play Factore hails itself as the “ultimate kid’s party venue” and it’s sure to keep your children entertained for hours.

Located in Manchester next to the Trafford Centre, the mammoth play centre is open from 10am to 6pm and it’s designed for the whole family to enjoy.

Daredevils can brave the largest indoor slide in the UK, which stretches over 40m long.

The Big Red Slide can be used by kids aged over five, and adults can even do it too.

If you have a need for speed, you can also race your family and friends to see who can reach the bottom first. 

More on family days out

Play Factore also has the largest play frame in the UK, and you can explore tunnels, slides and numerous obstacles, including giant bubble balls, spider nets and rollers.

Sports lovers can take advantage of the inbuilt basketball court and football pitch - and it’s perfect for parents with energetic kids.

The soft play centre is also a fun haven for toddlers, and there is a whole section designed for tiny family members below the age of five.

They can explore three slides, soft shapes, tunnels and tricycles, among other exciting features.

And there are baby changing facilities located close by.

If your kids never seem to run out of energy, they can bounce to their heart’s content on the ValoJump trampolines, which allow you to play games at the same time.

Star Wars fans old and young alike will love their new indoor laser tag arena, which has been “transformed into a galaxy far, far away.”

And Play Factore even has its own arcade, complete with race bikes, air hockey, super whack-a-mole and arcade shooters.

Hungry guests can visit the venue’s Italian coffee shop and pizzeria, and food can be washed down with Kimbo coffee and Tango Ice Blast.

Adults can go to Play Factore from just £4.95, while kids aged five and above are £12.95 to £19.95, youngsters aged one to four are £9.95 and under six months go free.

Prices can vary depending on off peak and peak times.

For an additional cost of £5.50, you can check out the WePlayVR pod, which includes a game where you battle in a quest to destroy drones taking over the galaxy.

If that doesn’t keep the kids quiet, there is also a zip wire experience which lets you soar above your fellow playmates.

This costs £4.75 at peak times for those between five and 16 and includes a full safety briefing.

One mum wrote on TripAdvisor: "My daughter had her party at Playfactore. It’s was so good, the kids loved it and the party host Leila was AMAZING!!!

"The party rooms are great with music and disco lights. Really recommend for a stress free, fun party."
