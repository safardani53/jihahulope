What time is Bigheads on ITV tonight, who's host Jason Manford and which celebrity heads are feature

Publish date: 2024-03-17

ITV is known for airing madcap shows in a bid to provide evening entertainment for the whole family.

And its new off-the-wall game show, Bigheads, is no differnt, with giant model heads of the world's biggest stars competing in bizarre physical challenges.

When is Bigheads on ITV?

The sixth and final episode of the show airs tonight - May 28 - at 7pm on ITV.

Bigheads is a new ITV comedy game show which started on April 23 at 7pm.

Each week Jason Manford presides over eight members of the public wearing giant model heads of celebrities and competing in a series of bizarre physical challenges to win up to £10,000.

Talking about the show to TV Magazine, host Jason Manford said of the show: "I don’t think there is any such thing as too mad.

"Don’t get me wrong, it is bonkers, and in the first 10 minutes, you will work out whether it’s for you or not, but I think it’s good and there is room for it."

Who is Bigheads host Jason Manford?

Mancunian stand-up comedian Jason is a panel show regular and his UK tour dates often sell out.

Inspired by the likes of Billy Connolly, Eddie Izzard and Peter Kay, Jason first started experimenting with stand-up when working as a glass collector in a pub aged 17.

Jason got his big break through Johnny Vegas’ excellent, psychedelic sitcom Ideal, in which he played Jack, and he also hosted a breakfast show on Xfm Manchester.

Then in 2007 he was made a team captain on 8 Out Of 10 Cats and got his own show in the form of Channel 4’s Tonightly.

Which celebrities will feature on Bigheads?

Some of the Bighead celebrities featured on the six-part series are Simon Cowell, Mo Farah, Adele, Benedict Cumberbatch, Victoria Beckham, William Shakespeare, Boris Johnson, Prince Charles, Queen Victoria and Donald Trump.

Jason admitted about the show: "It’s basically me with Jenny Powell and Kriss Akabusi, who are the commentators on the show, laughing at people getting hit while wearing Donald Trump’s head."

He continued: "I really hope someone tweets Trump a picture of his Bighead and that he tweets back about it. I want a 4am tweet, in block capitals, from Trump talking about how it is not funny. He isn’t really known for his sense of humour, but that would be the greatest advert for the show.”
