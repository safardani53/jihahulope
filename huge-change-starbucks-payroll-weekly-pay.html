I work at Starbucks - the chain just announced a huge change and everyone is saying the same thing

Publish date: 2024-08-31

STARBUCKS has announced a huge change in for its employees.

A TikToker's video about the matter has drawn a lot of attention and the internet has split opinions on this new business practice.

TikToker "Hen," @kinghennn, posted a recent video captioned, "I don't know how I feel about this just yet."

With a wide-eyed face, he quickly cuts between his expression and a letter in the kitchen of what is presumed to be a Starbucks store.

The top of the notice highlights the company's new "Weekly Payroll Frequency."

It goes on to state that the stores in California, Massachussetts, Maine, and Minnesota will be making the switch.

"Many partners have shared that moving to a weekly pay cycle can help you better plan and support your financial needs," the letter read in part.

For Californian Starbucks "partners" this will take effect on 12/5/22.

Massachussetts, Maine, and Minnesota will see their first weekly check on the week of 12/12/22.

The internet was polarized by this change to the famous fast-food coffee shop's employee practices.

"Weekly paycheck are literally why my husband and I are in debt, it’s not as fun as it sounds," one comment read.

Another user apprehensive to such a tempting financial change said, "I like being 'broke' and waiting for one big check every 2 weeks now imma get small checks every week…. Dangerous."

Citing a tax issue, a third comment said, "Getting paid weekly is the best… but they take more money from ur taxes."

Doubts about the company's communication came from one presumed Starbucks employee.

They said, "I love that I found out about this through TikTok because my SM didn’t tell any of us.

On the flip side, one user of the TikTok platform was moved to work at the chain.
