Publish date: 2024-06-12

IF you're looking forward to having a garden full of family and friends this summer, you'll need a good quality gas BBQ.

Gas barbecues are an investment and can fetch up to thousands, but you can also find plenty of bargain buys that will get the job done.

We've hunted for some of the best cheap gas BBQs that'll cook up an impressive feast – without breaking the bank.

When it comes to the best barbecues, many people swear by gas rather than a charcoal grill for sheer convenience. There's no waiting for the charcoal to ignite: simply fire up your gas BBQ and away you go.

They're also easier to clean, create direct and consistent heat, and emit less carbon monoxide and soot into the atmosphere.

There are many different types of gas barbecues - from portable and tabletop options to bigger models with multiple burners, warming racks and side tables for food prep.

You can also find BBQs with built-in thermometers to help you monitor your cooking for better results.

How do I choose a gas BBQ?

Size is the number one consideration when it comes to choosing the best gas barbecue for you.

If you have a large garden and plan to cook for lots of people, there’s no reason not to go extra-large, although, of course, the price tag will be higher.

For larger barbecues, you also need to think about the number of burners it comes with. The more burners it has, the more people you can cook for at the same time and the more versatile the machine is.

But if you only have a small patio, or will generally only cook for a few people, look for something small and compact but with at least two burners.

You can also get portable barbecues that are great for camping trips.

Once you’ve decided on the size and number of burners, it’s time to think about additional features.

Some barbecues have a side burner that allows you to cook or warm other food at the same time, just like a hob at home.

While others feature a “flavouriser bar” or lava rocks, basically an extra surface over the gas that catches all the fat, sauces, and juices from the food.

When these evaporate, they help recreate the smoky barbecue flavour that you get in charcoal barbecues.

If you might need to move your barbecue around your garden, or in and out of storage, it’s worth going for one with wheels too.

And of course, you should look for hooks, rails and shelves in convenient places that will let you hang tongs and towels or rest plates of food.

How much do gas BBQs cost?

Prices for gas barbecues can vary enormously, from less than £100 to several thousand. It all depends on whether you’re buying branded versions and what features are included.

As proven by our list of cheap gas BBQs, you can get yourself a decent-sized barbecue for around £200 if you hunt for a bargain.
