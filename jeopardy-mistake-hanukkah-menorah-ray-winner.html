Jeopardy! fans accuse game show of 'mistake' in Holiday category and say the writers jumbled a relig

Publish date: 2024-05-20

JEOPARDY! fans have accused the iconic game show of flubbing a question about the Jewish tradition of Hanukkah on Friday's episode.

The contestants were asked to name the "wrong" candleholder used to celebrate, multiple heated viewers claimed.

2-day champ Ray Lalonde, a scenic artist from Ontario, Canada, faced Neal Gettling, a financial analyst from Chicago, Illinois, and Elizabeth Pontefract, a strategy consultant from Seattle, Washington.

Host Ken Jennings, 48, presented the video clue for $800 under the category "Christmas and Hannukah."

The screen displayed two men lighting an eight-candle lamp with a Jewish star in the middle and a ninth candle to light them.

Ken read over the clip: “While we talk about the 12 days of Christmas Hanukkah is celebrated over eight nights with lighted candles in this special candelabra.”

Ray - who won with $28K and a two-day total of $60,600 responded: “What is a Menorah” and was deemed correct.

Jeopardy! fans, however, flooded internet forums claiming that "Hanukiah" was the correct response.

A "Menorah" is a colloquial generalized term, but it's not what's actually lit to observe Hanukah, they said.

'TECHNICALLY, IT'S A HANUKKIAH'

One Reddit user wrote: "Hmm, technically that Menorah answer should be a Hanukkiah..."

"My exact thought," another viewer agreed.

A third wrote: "There was a mistake in the Christmas/Hanukah category. Menorah is not the correct answer. The correct answer is 'A Hanukiah.'"

"The $800 'question' in the category Christmas and Hanukkah was incorrect. We don’t light a menorah, we light a Chanukkiah," a fan wrote on Twitter.

Another Twitter user wrote: "UGH UGH UGH @Jeopardy had a clue where the answer was Chanukiyah and they called it a menorah."

"No one is going to ever guess the former. And J knows that," someone replied and they wrote back:

"So they just wholeheartedly allow a wrong answer? It's not like it's a show for people to know esoteric trivia or anything!"

As TIME described: "A menorah, which has only seven candleholders, was the lamp used in the ancient holy temple in Jerusalem — now a symbol of Judaism.

"A 'Hanukkiah,' however, has nine candlesticks — one for each night of Hanukkah and an extra one to light the others.

"According to strict Jewish law, a Hanukkiah should have eight candleholders of the same height and a ninth branch that is set higher than the rest."

So not only did the show seem off the mark here, but it's unclear where they found an eight-candle lamp as a Menorah has seven, a Hanukkiah nine.

Eventual winner Amy Schneider's response of "Hebrews" was accepted, while Sam Buttrey was denied a win for saying "Romans."
