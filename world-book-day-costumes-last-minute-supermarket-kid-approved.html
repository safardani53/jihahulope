World Book Day costumes you can pick up last minute in the supermarket - and they're all kid-approve

Publish date: 2024-06-25

NEED a last-minute World Book Day costume? You’re not alone.

Over a fifth of us find outfit sourcing more overwhelming than getting ready for the first day of school, according to Tu at Sainsbury's.

While 81 per cent of parents admit to feeling the pressure to deliver a good costume. 

But we’ve got your kids-costume conundrums covered. 

Here Clemmie Fieldsend picks the supermarket outfits from just £9.99 and asks Jude Thomas, six, from Manchester and Gwendoline Haswell, seven, from Chertsey, Surrey to give them their verdict just in time for Thursday.

Aldi

Best for Unisex

Jude wears Mandalorian, £9.99; shoes (worn throughout), £10, Primark 

Jude says: “I really like this one, I like the mask and the shoulders.
“The buckle is cool but I wish it had a sword or something with it.”

Rating: 4/5

Gwen wear Harry Potter cape and top, £9.99; leggings, £3 for two pack, socks £3 and shoes, £12, (worn throughout), Primark 

Gwen says: “I’m not really into Harry Potter but I like this costume.
“I like the wand and the glasses the most.” 

Rating: 3/5

Best for Comfort 

Jude wears Where's Wally, £16

Jude says: “I prefer the Mandalorian one because I like superheroes.

“This is okay because its very comfy and I can move around a lot.”

Rating: 4/5

Gwen wears Alice in Wonderland, £16

Gwen says: “I really like this one.

“I like the blue colour and the dress and the headband is pretty, too.”
