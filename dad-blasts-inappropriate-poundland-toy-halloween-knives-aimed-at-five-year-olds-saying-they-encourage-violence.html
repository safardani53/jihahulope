Dad blasts 'inappropriate' Poundland toy Halloween knives aimed at five-year-olds saying they encour

Publish date: 2024-05-10

A TAXI driver has slammed Poundland for selling fake Halloween knives that spurt blood to kids as young as FIVE - claiming it encourages them to go out and stab people in the real world.

John, who has asked us not to use his last name, blasted the budget retailer for pushing the products ahead of Halloween on Monday because they promote violence.

The 50-year-old's wife, Joanne, spotted the gruesome accessories in the store in Folkestone, Kent, on Friday and immediately complained to shop floor staff.

The 45-year-old merchandiser was told to take up her complaint with headquarters, which she did, but claims they "didn't want to know" and didn't seem to take her concerns seriously.

Dad-of-two John told The Sun: "My wife noticed them when she went into the Folkestone Poundland today to get some shopping bits. We just think it's morally wrong to be selling something like that.

"The knives are retractable and come with a load of fake blood.

"Any parent who buys that for their kids is just irresponsible.

"My wife mentioned it to the staff but they said she had to contact head office but they just said 'we'll look into it' and that was it. They didn't seem to take her complaint very seriously."

The couple, who moved to Kent to get out of Tottenham, north London, because of its rising crime rate, have two children aged 22 and 24.

According to the Metropolitan Police website in August this year there were 194 crimes in that area, increasing to 220 in September.

John added: "I don't think it is right at all. We used to live in a place where stabbings were rife. The packet says it is suitable for ages five and up which is just disgusting. I know kids like a prank and a joke but thinking it is a joke to stab someone is not funny, it is morally wrong."

A spokeswoman for Poundland said: "We can confirm the item is part of our current toy Halloween department. We always welcome customer feedback and as a result of the customers comments, can confirm this product will be reviewed against our range for next year."

This week Poundland has had to recall a number of its other Halloween products from sale after it emerged that they posed a fire risk.

Halloween wigs, hair clip pieces and witches hats have all been withdrawn and anyone who bought them urged to take them back for a full refund.

On its website the shop has a number of Halloween costume accessories for sale, most of which are now only available in stores after selling out, including a butcher's hook, zombie axe and toy chainsaw.

For those really strong of stomach, there on even Halloween "limbs" on offer.
