Phil Scott (Politician)- Net Worth 2023, Wiki, Age, Wife, Children

Publish date: 2024-08-23

Philip Brian Scott (born August 4, 1958) is an American politician who has served as Vermont’s 82nd Governor since 2017. He won the 2016 general election as a Republican with 52 percent of the vote. He was re-elected to a second term by a larger margin in 2018. He previously served as Vermont’s 80th Lieutenant Governor from 2011 until 2017. He was a State Senator representing the Washington County District from 2001 to 2011, prior to becoming Lieutenant Governor. He graduated from Barre’s Spaulding High School in 1976 and from the University of Vermont in 1980, where he got a Bachelor of Science degree in Industrial Education.

Phil Scott’s Career

According to Phil’s business career, after graduating from high school, Scott started working at DuBois Construction, a Middlesex company founded by his uncle. Scott joined the company as a co-owner in 1986.
Scott has served as President of the Vermont Associated General Contractors. A fire at DuBois Construction on January 6, 2012, caused significant damage, but the owners were able to rebuild and continue operations.
Scott sold his portion of the company after being elected governor to avoid any conflicts of interest, as DuBois Construction does business with the State of Vermont. He sold his 50% stake for $2.5 million plus 3% interest paid over 15 years.
Scott stated that in order to protect the firm’s bonding capacity, he chose to fund the sale himself rather than having the company borrow the money to pay him in full. Critics claimed that Scott’s sale of his company part did not totally eliminate potential conflicts of interest, but Scott and the attorney who arranged the sale on his behalf disagreed.
