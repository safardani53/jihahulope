Al Horford: Net Worth &amp; College [2023 Update]

Publish date: 2024-02-05

Al Horford is a professional basketball player active in the National Basketball League. Currently, he serves the Boston Celtics as a center and power forward. 

The Atlanta Hawks drafted him in the first round of the 2007 NBA Draft. Thereafter, the Hawks traded him to the Celtics in 2016, where he remained till the 2019 NBA season. 

Following the 2019 season, the Celtics traded him to the Philadelphia 76ers, who traded him to the Oklahoma City Thunder for the 2020–2021 NBA season.

Recently, in June of 2021, the Thunders again traded him to the Boston Celtics. 

In the league, the basketballer has been an NBA All-Star five times.

Likewise, the power forward was named to the NBA All-Rookie First Team in 2008 and the NBA All-Defensive Second Team in 2018.

Moreover, in 2011, he was named to the All-NBA Third Team. Not to mention, the NBA player had an exceptional collegiate career with the Florida Gators. 

During his college days, he led the Gators to the NCAA championship twice in 2006 and 2007. Similarly, the player was also the Southeastern Conference Tournament’s Most Valuable Player in 2007. 

Besides that, Horford has represented the Dominican Republic on an international platform and assisted the team in winning a bronze medal at the 2011 FIBA AmeriCup.

Al Horford | Early Life, Family, and Education

Al Horford was born in Puerto Plata, Dominican Republic. His parents are Tito Horford and Arelis Reynoso, and he is the oldest of five kids.

Tito is a former NBA player drafted in the 1988 NBA Draft’s second round as the 39th pick by the Milwaukee Bucks. Hence, it is safe to think that the father influenced his oldest son career-wise. 

Moreover, he was the first Dominican-born player to play in the league. Likewise, Tito served as a center to the Bucks from 1988 to 1990. 

After that, he had a brief career with the Washington Bullets, now known as Washington Wizards. Following that, the center went on to play in France and Italy. 

At the same time, the former basketballer was also playing for the Dominican National team and in the Dominican semi-pro leagues. Before his professional career, he played collegiate basketball for the University of Miami.

He had an outstanding career with the Miami Hurricanes men’s basketball team. Not to mention, his school, Marian Christian High School, recruited him from the Dominican Republic. 

As a kid, Al used to follow his father around during his practices and games and became heavily attached to basketball shortly after. Along with Tito’s skills, the basketballer also adapted his father’s discipline and respect towards the game. 

Al Horford’s Mother

Furthermore, the NBA player’s mother has been a sports journalist since 1988 and has been a very supportive figure in his life. She listened to her son when he wanted to attend a basketball academy at six years old. 

Arelis even convinced the academy to make an exception for Al since the academy only enrolled seven years old and above kids. She always believed in his dream and did everything possible to ensure he could achieve it. 

Reynoso started scavenging for the best basketball programs for her only son when he was in middle school. She knew that it was essential for Al to attend high school in the United States to improve his skills even more. 

Hence, after a good talk with her ex-husband and his wife, they decided that Al would move to the US after finishing middle school.

As much as she wanted to be there with her kid, Reynoso knew that moving to the US would be substantial for her son’s basketball career.

Siblings

Tito’s love for basketball did not just rub on Al but all of his kids. All of his kids played basketball in school while Jon Horford and Josh Horford pursued it professionally. 

Jon Horford played collegiate for the University of Michigan from 2010 to 2014 and the University of Florida from 2014 to 2015. Following his graduation, he entered the 2015 NBA Draft. 

However, he went undrafted, and shortly after that, went on to play for Indios de San Francisco de Macorís, a professional basketball team based in the Dominican Republic.

Besides that, Jon has played for the Cleveland Charge and the Grand Rapids Gold of the NBA G League. Furthermore, the youngest Horfords, Josh and Christian, also work on their basketball skills relentlessly. 

On the other hand, Tito’s daughters have said goodbyes to their professional basketball careers and have opted out for different professions. Anna works in wealth management while Maria is a professional photographer.

Nonetheless, their love for basketball isn’t any less than their brothers. From a young age, their lives were heavily influenced by the NBA and basketball, and believe it to be “ingrained in” their “lives in many ways.” 

Education

Al attended Grand Ledge High School and played high school basketball with the school’s basketball team. Moreover, he was a star player on the team and was well-known for his exceptional skills. 

Likewise, the athlete currently holds seven school records and was named Class A Player of the Year in his senior year. Not to mention, Rivals.com called him the No. 7 power forward and the No. 36 player in the country. 

Following his graduation, he chose to take up the University of Florida on their scholarship offer. The Chicago Bulls head coach, Billy Donovan, coached the NBA player. 

Previously, Donovan also served as the head coach of the Oklahoma City Thunder. He is the recipient of the 2020 NBCA Co-Coach of the Year. 

Al’s presence in the Florida Gators helped improve the team’s performance almost immediately. Furthermore, he assisted the Gators to two NCAA Championships in 2006 and 2007.

Al Horford | Basketball Career

High School and Collegiate Career

The NBA player played high school basketball for Grand Ledge High School. He was an outstanding player and averaged 21 points, 13 rebounds, and five blocks per game as a senior. 

After high school graduation, Horford attended the University of Florida on an athletic scholarship. Upon his arrival, he assisted the Gators in winning the 2005 Southeastern Conference Tournament championship.

They won the title in 2006 as well. Thereafter, the Gators went on to win the 2006 NCAA championship after defeating UCLA in the finals. 

In the middle of his junior year, Al sustained an injury that caused him to miss many games. Following that, he was almost ruled out of a crucial game against the Ohio State Buckeyes. 

Nevertheless, he played and helped the Gators defeat the Buckeyes on December 23, 2006. In the final game on April 2, 2007, the Florida team became the first team since 1992 to win the NCAA Championship back-to-back.

NBA Career

Atlanta Hawks 

The Hawks drafted the former Gators player in the first round of the 2007 NBA Draft as the third pick overall. Shortly after, he signed a rookie contract with the team. 

He had shown the Hawks his worth in very little time, and his reputation indeed preceded him. Likewise, the athlete was named to the NBA All-Rookie First Team. 

Moreover, the Celtics center was a runner-up for Rookie of the Year honors. Not to mention, he was selected as the Rookie of the Month on four occasions. 

He helped the Hawks reach the playoffs as the fourth seed in the 2008-09 NBA season. The following season, the player earned his first NBA All-Star selection. 

Similarly, he was the first Hawks player since 1992 to earn the NBA All-Star selection. Thereafter, the basketballer signed a five-year contract extension worth $60 million with the team. 

Al earned another All-Star selection in 2011 as well. After performing exceptionally and assisting his team in winning the Shooting Stars Competition, he was injured in the 2011 NBA season. 

He suffered a tear in his left pectoral muscle. Nevertheless, the Godfather came back bigger and more vital for the 2012-13 NBA season.

Also, for the first time in his professional career, the center was named Eastern Conference Player of the Week. However, another injury slowed down his pace the following season.  

Nonetheless, after recovering from his injury, he earned his fourth and fifth All-Star selections back-to-back in 2015 and 2016. Since Horford’s contract was coming to an end, the Hawks tried to reach a new deal but couldn’t. 

Boston Celtics

As a result, the former Hawks player signed a four-year contract with the Boston Celtics. Following his appearance in the first three games as a Celtics player, Al suffered a concussion and missed a few games. 

Nevertheless, he earned his fifth All-Star selection with the Celtics. Thereafter, the athlete became an unrestricted free agent after his contract with the team ended.

Philadelphia 76ers, Oklahoma City Thunder, and Return to the Celtics

Following his career with the Celtics, Al signed a four-year contract with the Philadelphia 76ers. However, they traded him to the Oklahoma City Thunder after the 2019-2020 NBA season. 

On the other hand, the Thunders chose to sit him out for most of the 2020-21 NBA season as the team focused on developing young players. 

As a result, Horford requested a trade and made his way back to the Celtics.
