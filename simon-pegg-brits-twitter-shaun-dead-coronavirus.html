Simon Pegg and Nick Frost urge Brits to drink at home in #TwitterWinchester after Shaun of Dead coro

Publish date: 2024-02-23

SIMON Pegg has urged Brits to stay at home amid the coronavirus outbreak with #TwitterWinchester after releasing a Shaun of the Dead-themed advice video. 

Pegg, along with co-star Nick Frost, reprised his role in the hit horror comedy to tell the public to stay at home and follow NHS guidelines until the epidemic has passed.

In 2004, the pair played Shaun and Ed, two best friends who take refuge in their favourite pub, The Winchester, when a zombie apocalypse hits London.

The clip came ahead of an announcement yesterday by Prime Minister Boris Johnson that pubs, restaurants, and leisure centres across the country would be told to close in a bid to stop the spread of the coronavirus.

In contrast to the film, it sees Pegg urge Frost not to go drinking, and instead to "have a cup of tea and wait for all this to blow over".

"Do not go to the Winchester. The pub is out," he says.

Frost responds: "Even if it's shut?"

"Especially if it's closed," says Pegg.

"Remember what happened the last time?"

"Yes," says Frost, before mimicking the sound of a zombie trying to claw its way into the building.

As well as closing pubs, the government has this week taken the decision to shut down all schools and asked Brits to work from home if at all possible.

The UK currently has almost 4,000 confirmed cases of the coronavirus and at least 177 people known to have died.

Pegg concludes: "Above all, don't be a t**t about things.

"We're all in this together, don't be selfish.

"Look after each other, give someone a call if you think they might be lonely."

Following the government's announcement that pubs would be closing, Pegg tweeted: "The #TwitterWinchester is getting ready to open its doors. 

"Wherever you are in the world, come and raise a glass to the frontline medical personnel who never call time. Clinking beer mugs #Covid_19"

The message prompted a flurry of Twitter users to post their own clips showing them having a drink as part of the online gathering.

The week also saw the Queen tell Brits we all had a "vitally important part to play" in stopping the epidemic.

"We are all being advised to change our normal routines and regular patterns of life for the greater good of the communities we live in and, in particular, to protect the most vulnerable within them," a statement said.
