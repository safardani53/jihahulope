All About Kyrie Irving

Kyrie Irving, originally Kyrie Andrew Irving, is a professional basketball player who currently plays for the Dallas Mavericks of the National Basketball Association.

He was born in Melbourne, Australia, on March 23, 1993, to American expatriates Elizabeth Irving and Drederick Irving.

He grew up with his two siblings, Asia and London.

Growing up in West Orange, New Jersey, Kyrie played basketball at Montclair Kimberley Academy for his freshman and sophomore years.

After a sophomore year, he transferred to St. Patrick High School.

In 2010, Kyrie, as part of the United States national team, won gold at the FIBA Americas Under-18 Championship.

He played colligate football for Duke Blue Devils at Duke University, for whom he committed on October 22, 2009, in a TV broadcast on ESPNU.

Irving performed extremely well for the Devils, but in 2011 he announced that he would forgo his final three seasons of eligibility to enter the NBA Draft.

He was picked first overall by the Cavaliers in that year’s draft.

After seven seasons with the Cavs, Irving requested a trade, reportedly wanting to be more visible of his own team instead of staying in LeBron’s shadow.

On August 22, 2017, he was traded to the Boston Celtics.

Moreover, Irving signed with the Nets on July 7, 2019, and had been playing for the team until he requested his trade ahead of the NBA trade deadline on February 9, 2023.

He currently plays for the Dallas Mavericks. In fact, he led the team to victory against the Los Angeles Clippers on his debut game with his new teammates in front of a cheering Luka Doncic on the bench on February 8, 2023.
