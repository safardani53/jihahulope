Avani Lekhara Career

In 2012, Avani sustained a spinal cord injury in a serious accident. This confined her to a wheelchair. A few years later, her father encouraged her to participate in parasports. Her father suggested that Avani attempt both archery and shooting. However, she felt more at ease and assured when holding the rifle. This launched her career as a professional parachutist.

After reading his autobiography, she derives inspiration from Abhinav Bindra. She has achieved a bronze medal for the nation at the National Championship after only a year of training. In addition to a significant confidence boost, she now has superior coaches. She is coached by Suma Siddharth Shirur at the moment.

She has competed in various para-shooting competitions, including the World Championships and World Cups. In contrast, she finished ninth at the 2018 Para Shooting World Championships with a score of 1130. Nonetheless, she won a silver medal at the 2018 World Cup with a score of 244.

Avani Lekhara’s performance at the 2020 Tokyo Paralympics was extraordinary. She assisted India in winning the 10m air rifle gold medal. This accomplishment made her the first Indian woman to achieve a Paralympic gold medal. In addition, her total at the Paralympics set a Paralympic record and matched the world record.
