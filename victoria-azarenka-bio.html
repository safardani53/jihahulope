Victoria Azrenka Bio: Earnings, Endorsements &amp; 2023 Australian Open

Publish date: 2024-05-25

The Belarusian professional tennis player Victoria Azarenka is a former world No. 1 in singles and is a two Grand Slam singles title holder at the 2012 and 2013 Australian Open.

She has won a total of 21 WTA singles titles and claimed the world No. 1 title for the first time on 20 January 2012.

Victoria was the year-end No. 1 in 2012 and held the top ranking for a total of 51 weeks.

She recently competed in the 2023 Australian Open in Melbourne but lost to Kazakhstani professional tennis player Elena Rybakina in the semifinals on January 26, 2023.

Victoria had not played in the quarter-finals of a Grand Slam since 2016 because of her pregnancy, injuries, and off-court problems, but she’s slowly been returning to good form and has continued her performances from the end of 2022 through the Australian Open 2023.

 Early Life

Victoria Azárenka was born on July 31, 1989, in Minsk, Soviet Union, to her father, Fedor Azarenka, and mother, Alla Azarenka.

She grew up with her only sibling, Max Azarenka. Nevertheless, there is no information regarding his whereabouts.

Victoria always had a fascination for tennis. As a child, she idolized the German professional tennis player Steffi Graf.

This led her to pursue it as a career. Her real tennis persuasion came at age 15 when she moved to Scottsdale, Arizona, from Minsk to train.

After moving to Scottsdale, Nikolai Khabibulin, an NHL goalie, and his wife, Victoria Khabibulin, helped young Victoria with her training.

Career

On November 2003, Victoria debuted on the ITF Junior Circuit in Israel, winning one doubles title with Olga Govortsova.

Two years after turning pro, in 2005, she clinched two junior Grand Slam titles.

The same year ITF named her the 2005 World Champion making her the first Belarusian to do so.

She was seeded 26th at the 2008 Australian Open. It went well for the first two matches, but in the third, she lost to the defending champion Serena Williams.

However, she won the title in 2012 and 2013, becoming the first Belarusian player to win a Grand Slam tournament singles title.

Net Worth, Prize Money & Career Earnings

One of the greatest professional tennis players of all time, Victoria Azarenka, has a net worth of $20 million as of 2022.

She has earned an enormous amount through her super successful professional career. Aside from her prize money, Victoria earns well from her brand endorsement deals with companies such as American Express, Citizen Watch, Yonex, Wilson, Nike, and Six Star Pro Nutrition.

Forbes named Victoria the fourth highest-paid female athlete in the world in August 2013, with total earnings of $15.7 million.

Of the total amount, she made $6.7 million in prize money, and her endorsements equaled $9 million.

Similarly, her $7.9 million in prize money the year before was a single-year record for a female athlete in any sport, which still remains the 6th highest single-year total earned in WTA history.

All in all, she has made more than $34.76 million in prize money, which is 7th on the list of all-time WTA prize money.

Relationships, Children & Custody Battle

Beautiful Belarusian tennis player, Victoria Azarenka’s name has been linked to several famous men over the past years.

From 2011 and 2012, she dated the Ukrainian professional tennis player Sergei Bubka.

Similarly, she dated the American musician Stefan Gordy (Redfoo) from 2012 to 2014.

Following the breakup, she began seeing Billy McKeague, son of Chase McKeague (chairman of the investment group Chase and Company).

They even had a son on December 19, 2016, and named him Leo. However, they broke up in 2017, immediately after Wimbledon.

They not only split but became involved in a custody battle for Leo.

After some substantial legal back and forth, it was reported that Azarenka won her custody battle against McKeague in 2018.

Bizarre Drama At Australian Open 2023

The Belarusian tennis star Victoria was asked to take off a shirt she was wearing in a bizarre scene before her match against Elena Rybakina at the Australian Open 2023.

As soon as she walked out onto the court, the chair umpire Alison Hughes asked her to remove the PSG soccer jersey she was wearing.

She has previously explained how she wears the Paris Saint Germaine jersey for her son, who wants to play for the French club someday.
