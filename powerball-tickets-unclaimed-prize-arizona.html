Lottery warning to check Powerball tickets for $100,000 unclaimed prize and it was bought at a resta

Publish date: 2024-06-28

LOTTERY officials are telling people to double check their Powerball tickets, as a $100,000 prize remains unclaimed.

The winning Powerball lottery ticket was recently sold in Fountain Hills, Arizona at a restaurant.

The Powerball ticket came from Parkview Tap House, a bar and grill, according to local ABC affiliate ABC15.

The winning ticket was for the Saturday, November 4, lottery drawing.

The winning numbers to look out for are 1, 28, 30, 34, and 52. The Powerball number is 6.

The lucky Arizona winner has 180 days from the draw date to claim their $100,000 prize.

This gives them until May 2, 2024 to claim the prize at an Arizona Lottery Office.

If unclaimed, the money will be allocated based on Arizona's lottery jurisdiction laws.

HOW TO PLAY

The Powerball is a national lottery that is drawn every Monday, Wednesday, and Saturday.

To play, you choose five regular numbers from one to 69, and one red Powerball number from one to 26.

You can either pick the numbers yourself or use the Quick Pick option, which chooses for you.

The unknown player won by matching all five numbers as well as the Powerball number to the numbers drawn on Saturday night.

However, there are nine ways to win money from the Powerball.

  • Match five white balls and the Powerball to win the grand prize.
  • Match five white balls to win $1 million.
  • Match four white balls and the Powerball to win $50,000.
  • Match four white balls to win $100.
  • Match three white balls and the Powerball to win $100.
  • Match three white balls to win $7.
  • Match two white balls and the Powerball to win $7.
  • Match one white ball and the Powerball to win $4.
  • Match the Powerball to win $4.

Before playing, keep in mind that gambling is inherently risky and can lead to addiction.

Lottery players who are struggling with gambling addiction can call the National Gambling Hotline at 1-800-522-4700 or visit the National Council on Problem Gambling website.

MORE UNCLAIMED WINS

Several other unaware lottery winners have failed to claim their winning ticket.

In North Carolina, a $270,000 prize remains unclaimed.

The Carolina Cash 5 ticket was bought at a gas station in Charlotte.

The ticket is set to expire this upcoming Monday, November 13.

Another lottery winner in Cumberland, Rhode Island has yet to claim their $1 million prize.

Purchased in July, the winner still has a few months to claim their win.
