John Brzenk | Early Life, Family, & Childhood

John, the Giant Crusher, was born on July 15, 1964, in McHenry, Illinois. Like John, his dad John Brzenk Sr. was also an arm wrestler.

The former athlete states that he gained his forearm size through genetics from his father, Brzenk Sr. So, it is fair to say that John got into sports because of his father’s influence. 

Sadly, nothing much information is available regarding his mother, other family members, early life, and education because of his secretive lifestyle. However, what’s known is that John has a little brother named Bill Brzenk. 

But it is known that John Brzenk started to arm wrestlers in grade 8. He had a broken arm when he arm-wrestled his father’s friend as an eighth-grader.

Nonetheless, it did not stop him from pursuing his career as a arm-wrestler, as he participated in his first tournament at 16. 

John earned the nickname the “Giant Crusher” for his exceptional ability to overpower an opponent twice his size.

John Brzenk | Arm Wrestling Career 

It was unusual to see a boy of a small frame defeat someone bigger than himself. However, the audience did not know that John would secure his name in the history of arm wrestling.

After competing in smaller events, John Brzenk won ABC’s 1982 Wide World of Sports, his first world title.

He was just 18 years of age at the time. His 16 inches right forearm and 13.5 inches left forearm were doing wonders in arm-wrestling.

The heaviest opponent he faced weighed around 660 lbs. But there are no opponents his arms couldn’t pin.

John has only lost a single super-match in his entire career.

Besides arm wrestling, John Brzenk works as a mechanic at Delta Air Lines. John states that the job does not require brute strength, so he gets plenty of rest. 

The Iron Fist

John Brzenk is also known as the man with the iron fist after his impressive wins against formidable opponents. He progressed continuously and won the Middleweight World Championship in 1995. 

Because of his legendary reputation, John earned a cameo in Sylvester Stallone’s movie ‘Over the Top.’ However, he was not credited for his appearance in the film. 

John Brzenk was already a household name by the early 2000s. On the other hand, the giant athlete was also contemplating retirement because of his old age. 

His endurance and strength in the arms were getting weak each year. Thus, numerous competitors worldwide began to catch up to him. 

The “Pulling John”

John’s legacy and achievements have been immortalized by a documentary made in 2009. The documentary ‘Pulling John’ was directed by Sevan Matossian and Vassiliki Khonsar. 

‘Pulling John’ gives an insight into the Zloty Tur Cup in 2009, which took place in Warsaw. The documentary is not only about brute strength but also mental battles.

It educates viewers with details about the happenings inside John Brzenk’s mind and perfectly showcases John’s personality and characteristics. 

Arm wrestlers were commonly arrogant and cocky as they had pride over their brute strength. You will find John as one of the most humble beings, despite being at the top of the game for 25 years.

He is a mild-mannered athlete, one of the many reasons why fans around the globe love and adore him. 

A Legacy Left Behind

John’s legacy has been written in history despite his loss against Alexey Voyevoda. The World champion lost to the Russian arm wrestler in the 2004 event in Warsaw. 

Brzenk’s first loss was against Alexey, which ended his 25-year winning streak. But John avenged his defeat in a rematch the following year. 

John has won the middleweight titles in 2015, 2016, and 2017 as per sportsindiashow.com.

He became Wal’s 196-225lb Champion (Right Hand) in 2015. However, John could not defend the respective title in 2016 because of his injuries.

John Brzenk’s career record shows that he had won over 500 titles by the time he retired. 

Giant Crusher’s best matches were against Larratt, Voyevoda, Michael Todd, etc. The legendary John Brzenk still participates in arm wrestling events even at 59.

Work After Retirement

John Brzenk unofficially retired because of his old age and his injury. Later, he was announced as one of the co-hosts for the “Pound for Pound Arm wrestling” YouTube podcast and was joined by the founder, Ryan Bowen. 

John was involved in predicting, giving reviews during the podcast, and providing the fans with general information about the sports.

He beat Khadzimurat Zoloev on February 10, 2022, to reclaim the world’s top ranking in the 225-pound division.

During the fourth installment of the international arm wrestling tournament East vs. West on August 6, 2022, John lost to Kazakhstan arm wrestler Kydrgali Ongarbaev in a right-handed supermatch.

In the same year, on 19 November, Giant Crusher demolished Oleg Petrenko in a right-handed supermatch in a dominant fashion, 3-0.
